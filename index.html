overview:

In this case study I observed and interviewed of Coke Freestyle users at Brown University. After analyzing the takeaways from both the observation and interview, created two personas for coke freestyle users. Using one of those personas, I created a storyboard describing a personas entire journey as coke freestyle users.

user analysis:

To gain a more in-depth understanding of Coke Freestyle users, I observed the interactions of 3 users. Following the observations I also interviewed the same three users. The interview questions are listed below. Click on any question to hide the summarized reponses.

Observation Takeaways:
  • User #1: alone during interaction, quick interaction, seemed familiar with the interface, quickly made selection without contemplation
  • User #2: joked around with friends while using machine, freind commented on their choices but did not effect selection, chose to get water
  • User #3: with friend, spent a lot of time on the selection page, made initial choice then switched
1. How often do you use the Coke Freestyle machine?

All interviews use the Coke Freestyle machine every time they visit the vending machine, it is located in. One user is now off of the University meal plan so their usage has waned.

2. What time of day do you typically use the Coke Freestyle machine?

The users typically use the machine around lunch, between classes, or for dinner.

3. Are you typically alone or with friends when you use Coke Freestyle machines?

One user is typically alone when using it, one is often is in line with friends and for the other it is roughly 50/50.

4. Why did you chose the Coke Freestyle machine over the other drink dispensers?

The other drink dispenser options on campus are much more limited and the Coke Freestyle machine has the best water on campus, the best temperature of drinks, and the most fun interface.

5. How and when do you determine what drink you get when using the Coke Freestyle machine?

One user will always get the same thing unless they are with people who influence them to get something different. The other two vary their drink choices depending on how they are feeling.

6. Do you typically use the Coke Freestyle machine before or after getting your food?

All users typically use the machine after getting their food.

7. Have you ever abandoned your selection or changed you mind while using the Coke Freestyle machine? If so, why?

Some users abandon their selection when something goes wrong during the interaction, and there is a long line.

Users will change their selection when their original option is watered down that day.

8. When using the Coke Freestyle machine, was there anything that made getting your drink easy or difficult?

The older version of the Coke Freestyle machine used to misclick and took a long time to register user selections creating a frustrating user experience.

9. Do you typically get the same drink or do you experiment with new flavors?

Two of the users stick to the same two drink options. The other one likes to experiment with their drink choices because the interface allows for it.

persona #1: Driven Donna

Based on these the takeaways described in the user analysis section, I constructed two personas: Decisive Donna and Social Sebastian.

Driven Donna:

More About Donna:

  • Driven Donna's main issue with the interface occurs when either her typical drink of choice is watered down, or the interface is causing her to have to spend more time at the machine then normal.
  • Donna represents the users who value consistency over variety when using the Coke Freestyle machine. Despite the wide range of drinks available, she prefers to stick to the same one.
  • Rather than valuing the machine for its customization features, she appreicates its reliability in delivering her favorite drink - when it works as expected.

persona #2: Social Sebastian Donna

More About Sebastian:

  • Social Sebastian loves how easy it is to talk to his friends while using the Coke Freestyle machine. Nothing about the interface is too confusing where he would have to end his ongoing conversations.He sometimes even lets his friends pick his drink when he is in the mood for something new.
  • Sebastian represents the users who both like to swtich up their drink choices from time to time and use the machine with friends. Sebastian's interaction with the machine is typically longer than an individual user because he gets distracted by his friends.
